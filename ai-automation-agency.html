Observations

  • The same five to eight technical setup questions are being asked repeatedly by different members with no sign they found the answer in existing content, which means either the training videos are not discoverable enough or they do not cover these specific failure points, and this is creating friction that likely kills momentum for new members right before their first client launch.

  • International members represent a meaningful and growing share of posts but the core training is built around US infrastructure and compliance, leaving UK, South African, Dutch, Spanish, and Australian operators to crowdsource workarounds from each other rather than getting a definitive answer, which is both a retention risk and an untapped content opportunity.
